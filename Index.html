Background

We are working with a Sac State Physical Therapy professor to develop a microcontroller-based monitor of crutch usage on a crutch to be used by amputees. A load cell in the crutch monitors the force versus time. The monitor stores two weeks of data sampled at 1500 samples/second onto an SD card. We looking for a team to develop a user interface for the physical therapist to let him configure and start the crutch monitor and then to upload the data to a PC to graphically display it and generate some summary statistics of the data.

Scope

The scope of this project is to collect data taken from force measurments of a patient and then graphically display this data. One of the main parts of the business problem that will be addressed will be developing a graphical interface by which the data gets displayed and utilized by the patient and their physician

Proposed Solution

Give a high-level overview of the proposed solution...... A microcontroller will collect pressure sensor data approximately 10 -15 times a second. That data will need to be converted from its hexidecimal form into a decimal representation. Once in decimal form, our team will graphically display this data so that it can be useful to both the cleint and the doctor. One way this data will be displayed will be through through force calculations based on upon intervals of time in which the patient is using the crutch. These pre-set thresholds will indicated interims in time in which the force was greater which would indicate detremental or harmful use that should be corrected to ensure the patients health. This data will be uploaded into a database based upon the patients information so that the doctor can take steps to help his patients

Mission Statement:

Brute force aims to give medical professions data which would otherwise be hidden variables in the rehabilitation process of amputees. Brute force achieves these goals by collecting, visualizing, and analyzing the data acquired from patients devices. Doing so gives medical professionals accurate data in regards to the usage of a patient's devices and can better equip those professionals to give more personalized treatments to each patient. We hope that our process will be able to aid amputees in recovering faster and getting them back on track to living a full and healthy life as an amputee.
